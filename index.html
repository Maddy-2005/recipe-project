Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Indian Recipe Finder.

What types of Indian recipes can I find here?
+

You can explore a wide variety of authentic Indian recipes including North Indian classics like Butter Chicken, Biryani, and Paneer Tikka Masala; South Indian dishes like Dosa and Idli; street food favourites like Samosa; festive sweets like Gulab Jamun and Kheer; and vegetarian staples like Dal Makhani and Aloo Gobi.

How do I create an account?
+

Click the "Create an account" link above, or go to the Register page. Enter your name, email address, and a password of at least 6 characters. Once registered, you can log in immediately with those credentials. Your data is stored securely in your browser's local storage.

I forgot my password — what do I do?
+

Since all data is stored locally in your browser, there is no password recovery via email. If you have forgotten your password, you can register a new account with a different email address. We recommend noting your credentials in a safe place.

Is my account information safe?
+

Your account data (name, email, password) is stored only in your browser's localStorage — it never leaves your device and is never sent to any external server. This keeps your information private, but does mean your account is only accessible from the same device and browser you registered with.

Where does the recipe data come from?
+

All recipes are sourced from TheMealDB, a free public recipe database with thousands of dishes from cuisines around the world. When you search for Indian food, we query their API and display matching results with full ingredients, cooking instructions, and YouTube video links.

Can I use this app on my mobile phone?
+

Absolutely! Indian Recipe Finder is fully responsive and works beautifully on smartphones, tablets, and desktops. The recipe cards, search bar, modal popups, and all interactive elements are fully optimised for touch screens and smaller displays.

Do the recipes include cooking videos?
+

Yes! Many recipes include an embedded YouTube cooking video inside the recipe detail popup. When available, you will see a "Watch & Cook" section at the bottom of the recipe view where you can follow along with a video tutorial while cooking.
