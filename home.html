    Frequently Asked Questions

    Everything you need to know about using Indian Recipe Finder.

    How do the category tabs work?
    +

    Click any category tab (North Indian, South Indian, Street Food, Mughlai, Vegetarian, Indian Sweets, Indian Breads, Drinks & Rice) to instantly load a curated set of recipes for that cuisine type. Each category fetches real recipes from TheMealDB and shows them as cards. Click "View Recipe" on any card to see the full details.

    Why do some cards say "Local Favourite"?
    +

    TheMealDB is a global recipe database and doesn't include every regional Indian dish. When a dish like Pani Puri or Jalebi isn't found in the database, we show it as a "Local Favourite" card with a description. You can search for similar terms in the search bar to find the closest available recipe.

    How do I search for a specific dish?
    +

    Type any dish name in the search bar at the top of the page — for example "biryani", "palak paneer", "dal tadka", or "chicken tikka" — and press Search or hit Enter. You can also click the quick-suggestion chips below the search bar. Results from TheMealDB appear instantly as recipe cards.

    Do the recipes include cooking videos?
    +

    Yes! Many recipes from TheMealDB include a linked YouTube cooking video. When available, you will see a "Watch & Cook" section at the bottom of the recipe detail popup with an embedded video you can play directly on the page while cooking.

    How does logout work?
    +

    Click the "Logout" button in the top-right navbar. This clears your current session from the browser and redirects you to the login page. Your registered account stays saved in localStorage, so you can log back in at any time with the same email and password.

    Where does the recipe data come from?
    +

    All recipes are fetched in real time from TheMealDB — a free, open recipe database with thousands of dishes. Each search and category load makes a live API call, so you always get the latest available data including ingredients, instructions, and YouTube video links.

    Can I use this on my phone?
    +

    Absolutely! Indian Recipe Finder is fully mobile-responsive. The navbar, hero, category tabs, recipe grid, and recipe modal all adapt beautifully to small screens. The modal slides up from the bottom on mobile for a native app-like feel.
